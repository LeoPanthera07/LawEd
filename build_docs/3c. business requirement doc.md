## 1. Executive Summary

NyayaSetu is a legal intelligence platform addressing one of India's most persistent structural failures: the inaccessibility of the justice system for ordinary citizens. India has 1.4 billion people, three brand-new foundational laws (BNS, BNSS, BSA replacing IPC, CrPC, and the Indian Evidence Act in 2023–24), and no affordable, scalable digital system to help citizens understand and act on those laws.

NyayaSetu closes this gap by accepting a citizen's natural language description of their legal situation and returning a structured, expert-grade legal intelligence response — grounded strictly in BNS, BNSS, and BSA — along with a paid structured document the citizen can use to file an FIR or engage a lawyer directly.

This BRD defines the business requirements, stakeholder expectations, revenue model, KPIs, compliance obligations, and organizational dependencies that govern NyayaSetu's development and launch.

## 2. Business Problem

### 2.1 The Core Problem in Business Terms

India's legal system excludes the majority of the people it is designed to protect. The cost of entry — a lawyer consultation — starts at ₹2,000 and routinely reaches ₹10,000 for even preliminary guidance. For 800 million Indians living on under ₹10,000/month, this is not an option. The result is a market failure: legal need exists at massive scale, willingness to engage exists, but no affordable supply exists to meet it.

This is not a social problem dressed as a business opportunity. It is a genuine market gap — a service that 400 million legally active adults need, cannot access at current price points, and will pay for at the right price. NyayaSetu's business model is built on the premise that ₹10–₹29 per query and ₹99–₹299 per writeup is below the psychological resistance threshold for a citizen who has just experienced a road accident, property fraud, or workplace harassment and needs to know what to do.

### 2.2 The Structural Timing Advantage

The 2023–24 transition from IPC/CrPC/Evidence Act to BNS/BNSS/BSA has created a 2–3 year knowledge vacuum. Existing legal platforms — LawRato, Vakil Search, MyAdvo — are lawyer marketplaces built on the old legal framework. None has rebuilt their intelligence layer around the new laws. NyayaSetu enters the market purpose-built for BNS, BNSS, and BSA — with no technical debt from the old framework. This is a first-mover advantage in legal infrastructure, which historically compounds into decade-long moats.

## 6. Business Rules

|ID|Rule|
|---|---|
|BB-01|A writeup can only be generated after a clause mapping has been completed for the same query|
|BB-02|A writeup can only be generated after payment is confirmed — no exceptions|
|BB-03|An escalated case cannot proceed to writeup generation until an evaluator lawyer has reviewed and approved it|
|BB-04|A citizen's personal data (name, contact) is collected only at writeup generation — not at query submission|
|BB-05|The Evaluator Lawyer role can only be assigned by an Admin — self-assignment is not permitted|
|BB-06|Free tier queries count against the monthly limit regardless of whether the user is satisfied with the result|
|BB-07|All platform output must include the legal disclaimer — this cannot be disabled by any user role|
|BB-08|Evaluator lawyers are not permitted to contact citizens directly through the platform — all communication is mediated through the case review system|
|BB-09|NyayaSetu does not provide legal representation — the platform explicitly and visibly disclaims any attorney-client relationship|
|BB-10|Revenue share with evaluator lawyers applies only to escalated cases where the citizen proceeds to writeup — not for review-only cases|

## 10. Assumptions and Dependencies

### Business Assumptions

- Citizens are willing to pay ₹10–₹29 for clause mapping when the value is demonstrated via free tier
- Citizens are willing to pay ₹99–₹299 for a structured writeup they can physically use at a police station or law firm
- At least one lawyer volunteer is available for Phase 1 escalation validation at zero cost
- The legal information vs legal advice distinction is sufficient to protect NyayaSetu from Advocates Act liability — legal counsel should confirm before launch
- BNS, BNSS, and BSA PDFs are in the public domain and can be used as a corpus without licensing restrictions

### Business Dependencies

- Razorpay merchant account approval before launch
- Groq API access and free tier availability for MVP LLM inference
- Railway and Vercel free tier availability for MVP infrastructure
- At least one Bar Council-registered lawyer willing to serve as evaluator for Phase 1
- DPDP Act compliance review completed before launch (can be self-conducted for MVP, legal counsel recommended pre-scale)
