# Chosen Product Approach Document

---
## 1. Chosen Approach Summary

**Product type:** Web application (mobile-first responsive, native apps in later phases)

**Target user:** Any Indian citizen who has experienced or is anticipating a legal situation — regardless of legal literacy, background, or the nature of their query

**Core value delivery:** The user describes their situation once in plain natural language — the way they would explain it to a trusted friend. The system understands the intent, retrieves the most relevant clauses and articles from BNS, BNSS, and BSA using a RAG pipeline grounded strictly in the source documents, and returns a single structured legal intelligence response: ranked clauses, exact statutory text, plain-English explanation, and contextual relevance — all without hallucinating a single reference.

**Business model:** Freemium to start (free tier: limited queries/day), subscription for citizens (unlimited queries, saved reports), B2B licensing for law firms (API access, structured reports, unique case ID handoff system)

---

## 2. Why This Approach

**Why it fits your profile and goals:** You are building for production, not for a portfolio. You have the RAG and LLM depth to execute this correctly, and you are at IIT Gandhinagar with the academic credibility to approach law firms and legal institutions as early partners. Approach 3 is the only one that matches the full ambition of your vision — a middleware between citizens and the Indian judiciary. A form (Approach 2) narrows you to specific incident types. A pure search layer (Approach 1) undersells the intelligence. This approach treats the user as a human, not a form-filler, and the system as an expert, not a search engine.

**Why it is better than the alternatives:** Approach 1 builds the retrieval engine but stops short of the intelligence layer — it is a component, not a product. Approach 2 solves guided UX but locks you into structured inputs, killing the universality that is your core differentiator. Any incident, any query, any citizen — that scope requires natural language understanding at the front, not a dropdown. Approach 3 is the only one that scales from a road accident to a property dispute to a workplace harassment case without changing the interface. The shortcomings are all engineering problems — hallucination prevention through retrieval grounding, speed through session-based builds with Claude Code, accuracy through strict source-anchoring. None of them are product problems.

**Why "not a chatbot" is your moat:** Every other legal AI tool in India right now is either a chatbot or a search bar. You are building a legal intelligence interface — one input, one expert-grade structured output. That product decision alone is a positioning moat.

## 3. Approach Snapshot

- A citizen describes any legal situation in natural language — one input, no forms, no dropdowns, no follow-up questions
- The system uses a RAG pipeline grounded strictly in BNS, BNSS, and BSA source documents — the LLM explains, it never invents clause references
- The output is a single structured legal intelligence response: top 5–10 ranked clauses with exact statutory text, plain-English explanation, and relevance context per clause
- The interface is not a chat thread — it is a legal intelligence tool; the output feels like a senior advocate handed you a structured brief
- This is the foundation layer — every future feature (law firm ID handoff, agentic plaintiff/defendant debate, case strength scoring, judiciary API integration) plugs directly into this core pipeline

## 5. Handoff Note

We are building a web-first legal intelligence platform for Indian citizens called — working title — **NyayaSetu** (bridge to justice). The core product is a single-input, single-output legal intelligence interface: a citizen describes any legal situation in plain natural language, and the system returns a structured expert-grade response mapping the situation to the most relevant clauses and articles across BNS (Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita), BNSS (Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita), and BSA (Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam) — with exact statutory text and plain-English explanation per clause, strictly retrieval-grounded to prevent hallucination. The stack is FastAPI + Next.js + PostgreSQL + pgvector + Groq (Llama 3). This is not a chatbot. It is a legal intelligence tool. The MVP is the clause mapper. The roadmap includes: law firm case ID handoff, agentic plaintiff/defendant debate with a judge agent for case strength scoring, and integration with Indian judiciary public records. The chosen approach is Approach 3 — conversational natural language understanding with RAG retrieval and structured output display. Take this document as input and begin idea refinement.
